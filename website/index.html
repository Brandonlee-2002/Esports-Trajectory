Modeling Professional Player Career Trajectories in League of Legends Esports

CS163 Senior Capstone Project

Project Summary

This project analyzes career trajectories of professional League of Legends players across regions and competitive tiers. Using publicly available esports records from Leaguepedia, we study how long professional careers last, how players progress from lower-tier leagues to top-tier competition, and what factors are associated with career longevity.

Through survival analysis, regression modeling, and statistical testing, we quantify career patterns and identify regional differences in player development systems. Our findings provide insights for players, teams, and league administrators.

Broader Impacts

  • Player Career Planning: Help aspiring professionals understand realistic career expectations and make informed decisions about pursuing esports.
  • Team Management: Provide data-driven insights for roster building, player development investment, and talent scouting strategies.
  • League Development: Identify effective regional structures that support longer, more sustainable professional careers.
  • Industry Growth: Contribute to the maturation of esports as a profession by establishing baseline career metrics comparable to traditional sports.

Data Sources

  • Leaguepedia (lol.fandom.com) - Primary source for player profiles, career timelines, and team history
  • Liquipedia - Supplementary tournament and roster data
  • Games of Legends (gol.gg) - Primary source for player performance statistics from Seasons 6 to 15, inclusive

Dataset Overview

Our dataset covers professional League of Legends players across Seasons 6-15 (2016-2025), tracking player appearances, performance metrics, and career duration.

Data includes: Player name, games played, win rate, KDA, and 20+ performance statistics per season. Career length is derived from the number of seasons a player appears in.

Note: Regional and role data is currently limited to Season 6 players only. Full regional breakdown will be available when enriched data is added for all seasons.

Research Questions

  1. Hypothesis 1 - Career Length: What is the typical length of a professional League of Legends career, and how does it compare to traditional sports?
  2. Hypothesis 2 - Tier Transitions: How often do players successfully transition from Tier 2 developmental leagues to Tier 1 professional play?
  3. Hypothesis 3 - Regional Differences: Do career lengths and development patterns differ significantly across competitive regions?

Major Findings Preview

  • Professional esports careers show a right-skewed distribution, with median career length of 2 seasons.
  • Most players (70%+) have careers lasting 1-3 seasons, with few achieving 5+ season longevity.
  • Regional and tier transition analysis pending additional data enrichment.

See Hypothesis 1 for career length analysis. Hypotheses 2 and 3 are pending complete data.
