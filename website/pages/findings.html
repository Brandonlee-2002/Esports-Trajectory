Finding 1: What is the typical length of a professional League of Legends career, and how does it compare to traditional sports?

Career lengths are strongly right-skewed: most players have short careers (often 1–3 seasons), while a smaller group sustains multi-season longevity. This pattern is consistent with a high-turnover competitive environment where roster spots are scarce.

Finding 2: How often do players successfully transition from Tier 2 developmental leagues to Tier 1 professional play?

In the current Tier 2 debut panel, promotion to Tier 1 is not guaranteed for most players. Among those who do promote, timing tends to cluster early, which supports a “narrow window” interpretation for developmental pathways.

For the interactive Plotly version used in presentation, open Interactive Chart.

Finding 3: Do career lengths and development patterns differ significantly across competitive regions?

Regional differences are meaningful in the current sample: LCK tends to show the longest observed average careers among major leagues, while LCS shows shorter averages in the available coverage. These gaps should still be interpreted alongside missingness and cohort effects.

Finding 4: Non-trivial predictive modeling is operational (Cloud Run)

Production predictor

The website includes a model-backed predictor connected to a Cloud Run inference API. It returns expected career length plus survival probabilities from Cox and Negative Binomial models, not only static charts.

Open Predictor (Cloud Run) for the live demo interaction.

Interpretation and Caveats

How to read these results

  • Some estimates are sensitive to incomplete coverage in certain regions.
  • Tier mappings are rule-based and may require periodic refinement.
  • Recent cohorts are right-censored; younger careers are still unfolding.
