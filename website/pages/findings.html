Finding 1: Career Lengths Are Right-Skewed

Most professional careers concentrate in short durations, while a smaller set of players form a long right tail of durable multi-season careers. This supports a high-turnover structure in pro play.

Finding 2: Tier 2 Promotion Is Limited and Time-Sensitive

In the current roster panel, a minority of Tier 2 debuts reach a Tier 1 league, and successful transitions often happen early in a player's timeline. This indicates a narrow development window for promotion.

Finding 3: Regional Differences Are Statistically Meaningful

Career length differs by league context. Current tests indicate significant cross-region differences, with LCK generally leading in observed longevity and LCS showing shorter average durations in the available sample.

Finding 4: Non-Trivial Predictive Modeling Is Operational

Production predictor

The site includes a model-backed predictor page connected to a Cloud Run inference API. It returns expected career length plus survival probabilities from Cox and Negative Binomial models, not just static visualization outputs.

Interpretation and Caveats

How to read these results

  • Some estimates are sensitive to incomplete coverage in certain regions.
  • Tier mappings are rule-based and may require periodic refinement.
  • Recent cohorts are right-censored; younger careers are still unfolding.
