Visualizations

Career Length Distribution

Observation: Right-skewed distribution with most careers lasting 1-3 seasons. The mean (dashed line) is pulled higher than the median (dotted line) by long-career outliers. This confirms that professional esports careers are typically short, with longevity being exceptional.

Regional Career Length Comparison

Observation: LCK shows the highest average career length among major regions, followed by LEC and LCS. LPL, despite having the largest sample size, shows slightly lower average career length. Regional infrastructure and competition intensity may influence longevity.

Role-Based Career Length

Observation: Career lengths are relatively similar across roles, with Mid showing slightly higher average. This suggests that role-specific factors have less impact on career longevity than systemic factors like region, era, or organizational support.

Temporal Trends

Observation: Left panel shows player entry rates by season. Right panel shows mean career length by start year for retired players. Earlier cohorts show longer careers, which is expected since they had more time to accumulate seasons. Recent cohorts' careers are still developing.

Preliminary Insights & Hypothesis Formation

Key Findings

  1. Career Length Distribution: Right-skewed with median of 2 seasons, mean of 3.09 seasons. Most careers are brief, with longevity being exceptional.
  2. Regional Differences: LCK players show longest careers (4.83 seasons avg), suggesting regional infrastructure may support career longevity.
  3. Role Similarity: Minimal differences between roles (4.29-4.48 seasons), indicating systemic factors dominate over positional factors.
  4. Temporal Effect: Negative correlation between start year and career length, partly due to observation window but may also reflect industry maturation.
  5. Team Movement: Moderate correlation between num_teams and career length, suggesting career longevity involves multiple team stints.

Questions for Future Analysis

  • Are regional differences statistically significant after controlling for era and role?
  • Does starting in Tier 1 vs Tier 2 significantly affect career longevity?
  • Is there a survival curve pattern - when do most careers end?
  • Do performance metrics (KDA, win rate) predict career length?
  • How has career length distribution changed as the esports industry matured?

Hypotheses Formed

H1: Professional esports careers are significantly shorter than traditional sports, with most careers lasting less than 3 seasons.

H2: Players starting in Tier 2 have lower probability of reaching Tier 1 and shorter overall careers than those starting in Tier 1.

H3: Eastern regions (LCK, LPL) produce players with longer careers than Western regions (LEC, LCS) due to infrastructure differences.
