    About the Models

    How it works

    Two models are used in combination. The Negative Binomial Regression predicts expected career length as a count, accounting for the overdispersed distribution of career lengths. The Cox Proportional Hazards model estimates the probability of a career still being active after each season, controlling for league, role, and debut era simultaneously.

    Both models were trained on 803 players from LCK, LPL, LEC, and LCS across Seasons 6–15, with right-censoring applied to players still active in S15. The inference service runs on Google Cloud Run and responds in real time.

    Limitations

    • Models are trained on 57% data coverage — LCS predictions have lower confidence due to smaller sample size (n=83).
    • Debut era is a strong confounder: early-era players had more seasons available, inflating their predicted lengths.
    • Individual performance metrics (KDA, GPM) are not yet included as features — only structural variables are used.
