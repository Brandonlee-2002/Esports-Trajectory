Hypothesis 2: Tier 2 to Tier 1 Transition Rates

Research Question: How often do players successfully transition from Tier 2 developmental leagues to Tier 1 professional play?

Hypothesis: The majority of Tier 2 players never reach Tier 1 competition. Those who do typically make the transition within their first 2 years, suggesting a critical developmental window for player promotion.

Summary

89 Tier 2 Starters
4.5% Reached Tier 1
4.0 Median Seasons to Tier 1
50% Promoted Within 2 Seasons

Key Finding

Of 89 players who debuted in Tier 2 leagues across Seasons 6–15, only 4 (4.5%) ever reached Tier 1 competition — strongly supporting the hypothesis that the majority of developmental players never break into elite play. Among those promoted, 50% did so within 2 seasons while the other 50% took 6–7 seasons, suggesting two distinct pathways: rapid early promotion or long-term persistence. CSM (creep score per minute) was the only statistically significant differentiator (p = 0.041), with promoted players averaging lower CSM — potentially reflecting a team-oriented playstyle over individual farm efficiency.

⚠ Data Limitation

Current analysis covers 57% of player-seasons (2,517 of 4,411). Missing data is concentrated in LCS and LEC — the most active Tier 2→Tier 1 pipeline leagues. Promotion rates are likely underestimated and should be considered preliminary pending full data coverage.

Implications

Support for Hypothesis

  • 95.5% of Tier 2 starters never reached Tier 1 — strongly supporting the hypothesis that the majority of developmental players do not break into elite play.
  • Of those who were promoted, 50% did so within 2 seasons, supporting the "critical developmental window" prediction.
  • The remaining 50% took 6–7 seasons, suggesting a secondary pathway of long-term persistence.
  • CSM was the only statistically significant differentiator (p = 0.041) — promoted players had lower CSM, possibly indicating that team-first playstyles are valued over individual farming efficiency.
  • Support and Jungle roles had the only non-zero promotion rates, which may reflect the high impact and visibility of these positions in the competitive meta.

Next Steps & Limitations

  • Complete Leaguepedia data fetch to improve coverage from 57% to ~90%+ (especially LCS and LEC data).
  • Re-run analysis with the full dataset to validate preliminary findings.
  • Investigate whether mid-season promotions show different patterns than end-of-season transitions.
  • Analyze whether promotion rates have changed over time as the esports ecosystem matured.
  • Examine whether performance metrics in the season immediately before promotion are stronger predictors than career averages.
